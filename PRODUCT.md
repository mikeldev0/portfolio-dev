# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, technical leads, engineering teams, freelance clients, and small-business clients who need to quickly evaluate Mikel Echeverria as a backend and full stack developer. The portfolio should also support local companies in Navarra and Spain, plus international opportunities in Europe and Switzerland.

Visitors need to understand what Mikel does, which technologies he works with, what projects he has built, whether he is available, and how to contact him without having to parse unnecessary presentation copy.

## Product Purpose

This site is a personal brand and credibility asset for Mikel Echeverria. It exists to present professional identity, technical ability, project evidence, availability, and contact paths in a way that makes Mikel look like someone a team or client can confidently hire.

Success means the site communicates backend and full stack competence, practical product sense, interest in AI and automation, clean architecture, useful software, and reliable execution. Projects should act as the primary proof of skill.

## Brand Personality

Precise, technical, modern, approachable, clean, reliable, and slightly premium without feeling pretentious.

The voice should be direct, professional, and human: confident but not arrogant, technical but understandable, and friendly to Spanish and English readers.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS landing page style, overanimated portfolio effects, childish gradients, excessive glassmorphism, startup-bro hype language, fake visionary founder tone, copywriting fluff, visual noise that distracts from projects and skills, and anything that makes the site look like a template.

Avoid product-style UI patterns unless they directly improve usability. The portfolio should prioritize trust, clarity, and technical credibility over spectacle.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with evidence: make projects, experience, technologies, availability, and contact paths easy to find and evaluate.
2. Be technical without becoming obscure: use precise language and structure so non-specialist recruiters and specialist engineering leads can both scan the site.
3. Keep motion restrained: use subtle animation only when it improves polish, orientation, or feedback.
4. Preserve personal credibility: the site should feel authored by a practical developer, not generated from a template or framed as a generic startup pitch.
5. Optimize for confident contact: keep availability and contact visible, but never desperate or noisy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA. Prioritize readable contrast, keyboard-friendly navigation, reduced-motion support, a color-blind safe palette, and interface states that do not rely on color alone.
